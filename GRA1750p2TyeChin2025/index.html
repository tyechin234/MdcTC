Oatmeal Raisin Cookies

Your go-to cookie sanctuary is Dessert Delights, where whimsical flavors blend with handmade charm. Each bite, whether it's a traditional favorite or an original creation inspired by the desert, is baked to bring happiness, coziness, and a little adventure.

Ingredients

  • 2 sticks of room temperature butter
  • 1 cup of brown sugar
  • ½ cup plus 2 tablespoons granulated sugar
  • 2 large Eggs
  • 1 tablespoon of pure Vanilla extract
  • ½ teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1 teaspoon of baking soda
  • 1 tablespoon of Molasses
  • 1 ½ cups of all purpose flour
  • 3 cups rolled oats
  • ½ teaspoon freshly grated nutmeg
  • ½ cup raisins

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F. Grease 2 large cookie sheets or line with Silpat or parchment paper.
  2. In a large mixing bowl, beat the butter until creamy. Add the brown sugar and white sugar and beat until fluffy, about 3 minutes. Beat in the eggs one at a time. Add the vanilla extract.
  3. Mix the flour, salt, baking soda, cinnamon, and nutmeg together in medium bowl. Stir the dry ingredients into the butter-sugar mixture. Stir in the raisins and nuts. Stir in the oats
  4. Spoon out the dough by large tablespoonfuls onto the prepared cookie sheets, leaving at least 2 inches between each cookie.
  5. Bake until the edges of the cookies turn golden brown, about 10 to 12 minutes. If baking 2 cookie sheets at once, swap their positions on the racks mid-bake.
  6. Cool 1 minute on the cookie sheets. Then carefully remove them, using a metal spatula, to a wire rack. Cool completely. They will be quite soft until completely cooled. Store tightly covered at room temperature for up to 5 days.
